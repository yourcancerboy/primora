Batam is one of Southeast Asia's best-kept golf secrets — and for golfers from Singapore, it is remarkably accessible. Forty-five minutes by ferry, internationally-designed courses, sea views on nearly every fairway, and green fees that make Singapore courses feel like a mistake.

Here is everything you need to know about planning a Batam golf trip.

---

## Why Golfers from Singapore Choose Batam

The arithmetic is simple. A round at a comparable course in Singapore costs SGD 150–300+. In Batam, the same quality round costs IDR 400,000–700,000 — roughly SGD 35–60. That is often less than a single hole at home.

Beyond price:
- **Uncrowded fairways** — tee times are far easier to get than in Singapore
- **Sea views** — most courses are built along or near the coast
- **No waiting** — weekend mornings that would be fully booked in Singapore are walkable in Batam
- **Easy access** — the ferry from HarbourFront takes 45 minutes; most courses are 15–40 minutes from Batam Centre

---

## Best Golf Courses in Batam

### Tering Bay Golf & Country Club
Consistently rated among the top courses in Indonesia. 18 holes designed to international standards, sea views from multiple fairways, well-maintained greens year-round. The clubhouse is comfortable and the pace of play is excellent.

**Best for:** Serious golfers wanting a premium round at an affordable price.

### Batam Hills Golf Resort
A 36-hole facility — two full courses — making it ideal for golfers who want a full day of play. The courses wind through natural terrain with sea glimpses from the higher elevations. Clubhouse has a restaurant and changing facilities.

**Best for:** Full-day golf trips, groups wanting different course experiences.

### Palm Spring Golf & Beach Resort
Arguably Batam's most scenic course — set along the beachfront with ocean views on most holes. A little more relaxed in atmosphere than Tering Bay, but the course itself is well-maintained and the location is hard to beat.

**Best for:** Golfers who want the beach resort experience alongside the round.

### Kabil Golf Course
A solid option for groups looking for straightforward, well-priced golf. Less scenically dramatic than the top three, but reliable, uncrowded, and conveniently located.

**Best for:** Budget-conscious groups, warm-up rounds.

## Day Trip vs. Overnight Golf Package

### Day Trip (Most Popular)
Depart Singapore on the 8:00am or 9:00am ferry, play 18 holes, have a seafood lunch, and return on the 4:00pm or 5:00pm sailing. Door to door from Singapore: under 12 hours. Very achievable and very good value.

### Overnight (Recommended for Groups)
Arrive Friday evening, play Saturday morning, relax at the resort Saturday afternoon, check out Sunday and return. Two days, one night — enough time to play a proper round without rushing, have a good dinner, and recover before the week starts.

## Practical Notes for Golfers

**Clubs:** You can bring your own on the ferry — clubs are allowed as checked baggage (verify with the ferry operator when booking). Rental sets are available at all major courses if you prefer to travel light.

**Dress code:** Smart-casual golf attire is standard at all courses. Collarless shirts are generally not accepted at Tering Bay and Palm Spring.

**Best time to play:** June to September is the driest period. Morning rounds are recommended year-round to avoid any afternoon showers.

**Pace of play:** Significantly better than Singapore. Most groups complete 18 holes in 3.5–4 hours.
