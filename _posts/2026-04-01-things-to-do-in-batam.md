Batam packs a surprising variety of experiences into a compact island just 45 minutes from Singapore. Whether you are planning a two-day escape or a full week, here is everything worth doing.

---

## 1. Relax at Nongsa Beach

Nongsa on Batam's northeast coast is the island's most polished beach zone. Several upscale beach resorts line the shore, and the calm waters make it ideal for swimming and water sports. The view across the Strait of Singapore from here is genuinely striking — you can see the Singapore skyline on clear days.

**Best for:** Couples, resort stays, water sports.

---

## 2. Eat Seafood at the Waterfront

Batam's seafood scene is the reason many Singaporeans make the trip. Open-air restaurants along the waterfront areas of Batam Centre and Harbour Bay pile tables with live-catch crabs, tiger prawns, lobster, and whole fish — all priced far below what you'd pay at home.

**Recommended:** Piayu Waterfront, Batam Centre waterfront, and Harbour Bay seafood strip.

---

## 3. Play Golf at World-Class Courses

Batam is quietly one of Southeast Asia's best golf destinations. The island has over a dozen international-standard courses, most with sea views and first-class facilities — at a fraction of the cost of courses in Singapore.

Top courses include:
- **Tering Bay Golf & Country Club** — consistently rated among the best in Indonesia
- **Batam Hills Golf Resort** — 36 holes, great for full-day play
- **Palm Spring Golf & Beach Resort** — beachfront location, scenic fairways

For travelling golfers, Batam offers professional-grade courses with green fees that will make you wonder why you ever played elsewhere.

---

## 4. Take a Day Trip to Bintan

From the ferry terminals in Batam, fast boats connect to Bintan — another Riau Islands destination with pristine white-sand beaches and luxury resorts. A combined Batam–Bintan itinerary is well worth considering for a longer trip.

---

## 5. Visit the Barelang Bridge

The Barelang Bridge is an engineering landmark — a series of six bridges connecting Batam to four surrounding islands. The drive across it offers panoramic sea views and a few stops for seafood and fresh coconut along the way. It is a pleasant half-day outing from Batam Centre.

---

## 6. Shop at Nagoya Hill Mall

Nagoya Hill is Batam's most popular shopping district. The mall and surrounding area has electronics, clothing, souvenirs, and local goods at prices noticeably lower than Singapore. It is also home to a wide range of restaurants and a lively street food scene in the evenings.

---

## 7. Try Water Sports at Ocarina Beach

Ocarina Beach on Batam's western side is popular for jet skiing, banana boat rides, parasailing, and kayaking. The beach has a more local, laid-back feel compared to Nongsa, and it tends to be less crowded on weekdays.

---

## 8. Visit Harris Resort Waterfront

Even if you are not staying here, Harris Resort at Batam's Waterfront City area is worth a visit for its beach club and water activities. The resort connects to a ferry terminal from Tanah Merah, making it an easy first stop for visitors arriving on that route.

---

## 9. Explore Batam Centre's Night Market

In the evenings, the area around Batam Centre transforms into a lively stretch of street food stalls, local restaurants, and informal markets. It is an easy and cheap way to eat well and absorb a bit of local life.

---

## 10. Take a Cooking Class

A small number of local guesthouses and boutique operators offer half-day Indonesian cooking classes. Learning to make nasi goreng, rendang, and satay is a rewarding use of a morning — and a good story to bring home.

---

## 11. Snorkel Around the Outer Islands

Day trips from Batam to nearby smaller islands offer good snorkelling in clearer water than you will find on the main island. Local boat charters can be arranged — ask your hotel or contact us and we will sort it for you.

---

## 12. Golf Packages from Singapore

Batam's combination of easy access and excellent golf courses has made golf packages from Singapore one of the most popular trip formats. A round of golf, a seafood dinner, and a night at a resort — arranged as a single package — is hard to beat for value.

---

## 13. Spa and Wellness Day

Several hotels and standalone spa operators in Batam offer full-day wellness packages — traditional Javanese massage, body scrubs, aromatherapy — at prices that are remarkable compared to Singapore. Ideal as a rest day between more active activities.

---

## 14. Explore Maha Vihara Duta Maitreya Temple

One of the largest Buddhist temples in Indonesia, this is a striking sight — colourful, expansive, and quietly peaceful despite its scale. Worth an hour if you are interested in religious heritage or simply want something different on your itinerary.

---

## 15. Enjoy a Sunset Dinner Cruise

A handful of operators offer short sunset cruises from Batam's waterfront — perfect for couples or small groups looking for a special evening. The views of the island at golden hour from the water are excellent.

---

## 16. Visit Ocarina Park

Adjacent to Ocarina Beach, this open park area is popular with local families and has carnival rides, food stalls, and a laid-back weekend atmosphere. It gives you a genuine sense of daily life on the island.

---

## 17. Try Local Indonesian Food

Beyond seafood, Batam has excellent local Indonesian cuisine — nasi Padang, soto, mie goreng, martabak, and fresh fruit juices. The Nagoya and Batam Centre areas both have a wide range of warung (small restaurants) where you can eat well for very little.

---

## 18. Corporate Team Activities

Batam has become a popular destination for corporate retreats and team building. Many resorts offer dedicated team-building programmes including water challenges, cooking competitions, go-karting, and outdoor adventure activities.

---

## 19. Beach Resort Weekend

For many Singaporeans, the ideal Batam trip is a simple two-night beach resort stay — arrival Friday evening, full Saturday at the pool and beach, Sunday return. Well-appointed resorts at Nongsa or Waterfront City make this effortless.
